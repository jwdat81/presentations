What is a constitution?

  • It’s a set of rules about making rules, which is the law.
  • Who makes the laws? (legislative power)
  • Who puts the laws into effect and enforces them? (executive power)
  • Who settles disputes over the law? (Judicial power)
  • What is government not permitted to do to its citizens? (civil liberties)

What did Madison want?

Madison's Virginia Plan

  • Strong national government that could pursue national interests
  • Power to tax
  • Power to regulate commerce
  • Power to control the military
  • Veto all state laws
  • Representatives in both a House of Representatives and Senate based on size of population. (the more people, the more votes)

Republican Government (Representative Democracy)

  • Similar to Rome
  • Rule by the people through representatives

Madison's Opponents

  • Robert Sherman of Connecticut
  • Narrow national power
  • Modest ability to tax
  • Modest regulation of commerce

New Jersey Plan

  • Build on the Articles of Confederation
  • Each state gets one vote
  • No vetoe of state laws

Constitutional Convention of 1787

  • Shaped by a battle about Madison’s vision of a nation’s future & Sherman’s defense of the nation as it was.

Compromise #1

The Great Compromise

  • Madison wanted representation by the size of the state in both houses.
  • Sherman wanted equal vote for all states.

    • Result

      • House of Representatives: more people more votes
      • Senate: all states have equal votes

Compromise #2

The President

  • Madison wanted a strong, independent president.
  • Sherman wanted a strong, independent senate to check the president.

    • Result

      • Create a president with large powers & frustrating constraints, especially the senate (approval process)

Compromise #3

Slavery

  • George Mason wanted a to abolish slavery.
    • “Slavery is a slow Poison, which is daily contaminating the Minds & Morals of our People. Every Gentleman here is born a petty Tyrant. And in such an infernal School are to be educated our future Legislators & Rulers.”
  • Pierce Butler wanted slavery.

  • Result

    • Constitution protected slavery.
    • A slave would be counted as 3/5th person for allocating representatives in the House.
    • States could bring in slaves until 1808.
    • Fugitive slaves would be returned to their owners.

Conclusion

  • The founders created a republican government which is extremely complicated and hard to use!
  • Majority rules; however, the majorities are extrordinary (almost always requires 60 senators today)
  • Gridlock was built into the constitution; it exactly how the founders intended it.
  • It’s hard when we are divided, but very effectual when we are united.
  • Only works when we compromise.
