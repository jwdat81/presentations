Learning from history

  • The Greeks: organize the polis (community) and to foster excellence
  • Plato: Philospher-King: strong leader who knows the meaning of justice and acts justly
  • Aristotle: What types of governments exist and which type would work the best under most circumstances?

Learning from history

  • Monarchy: ruled by one man
  • Aristocracy: ruled by a few men
  • Democracy: the well-intentioned many; the constitution
  • Oligarchy: the rich, also being the few, also rule the state
    • oligarchy and democracy can be fused to create a well-functioning government or polity.

Learning from history

  • The Romans: Political and legal institutions
  • Polybius: Separation of powers and checks and balances.
  • Cicero: Political legitimacy flowed from the informed consent of the individual. Service to one’s community is the highest human purpose.
