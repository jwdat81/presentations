Madison's Dilemma

“In framing a government which is to be administered by men over men, the great difficulty lies in this: you must first enable the government to control the governed; and in the next place oblige it to control itself.”

The Design:

  • Government is hard to use when the government is divided.
  • Constitution only works when politicians use all of their skills and persuasion to compromise.

How do Americans participate in politics?

  • We take many forms of political participation for granted:
    • Voting
    • Contacting officials (calling, writing, emailing)
    • Social media
    • Petitioning
    • Joining political organizations
    • Lobbying
    • Rallies and protests
    • Litigation

Who Participates?

  • Spectators: Registered voters who mostly vote but otherwise don't participate.
  • Serious Players: Contact local officials, contribute money and work for candidates
  • Intensive Players join parties, work in campaigns, or run for office.

Serious and Intensive Don’t Represent You, probably.

  • More Partisan and Ideological than average voters.
  • Enjoy above average incomes and jobs than average.
  • Older than average.
  • Less often racial minorities.
  • .01% of donor contribute 24% of campaign contributions.

Back to the Design: It's the Institutions!

  • American Government was designed to manage and control participation.

    • “ The evils we experience flow from the excess of democracy. The people do not want virtue, but are the dupes of pretended patriots. ” - Eldridge Gary
    • The Constitution provides lots of venues to participate, but also many opportunities for your opponents to stop you.
