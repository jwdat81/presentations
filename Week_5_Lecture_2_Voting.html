Voting and Elections

  • The ideal for American elections: Prospective Voting.
  • “ The people will be represented; they ought therefore to choose the representatives. The requisites in actual representation are that the representatives should sympathize with their constituents; should think as they think, and feel as they feel; and that for these purposes should even be residents among them.&rdquo - George Mason
  • Prospective Voting: Under ideal circumstances, rational voters cast prospective votes for the candidates that offer future policies they prefer.

In Perspective Voting all voters are considered:

  • Well informed rational actors.
  • Carefully consider several candidates.
  • Vote for the candidate who will do what the individual voter thinks is best.

The Reality of American Elections

  • Well informed rational actors.
  • Carefully consider several candidates.
  • Vote for the candidate who will do what the individual voter thinks is best.
  • Voter turnout is worse for the midterms.

Voter turnout varies by state and country

Why is it lower in the U.S.?

    • We vote on Tuesday, and it's not a national holiday.
      • Voter registration requirements, such as 21 days before election day in KS.
    • Political culture
      • Turnout is low in the South
      • The South has a history of restricting voting rights.
    • Americans move around a lot.
      • 24% of Americans have moved from their city within the last five years.
    • Americans vote frequently

Backtracking

    • Perspective Voter Theory
      • Perfect information .
      • Careful consideration of several candidates
      • Vote for the candidate who the individual thinks is the best.
    • Not exactly realistic!

Voter Turnout

    • Voter turnout is low and those who do vote don’t represent voting age adults.
      • Strong partisan identification
      • Higher incomes
      • Whiter
      • Older
      • Those who do not vote could change the outcomes of elections.

The Reality

  • We don’t meet the assumptions.
    • We vote based on many factors, many of which may not reflect perfect information or rational self-interest.

We Vote based on:

  • Party Identification
  • The image of candidates
  • Candidates stand on major issues
  • Incumbents past performance in office.

1. Party Identification

  • 62% still identify with major parties.
  • 93% of registered voters lean Democrat or Republican.
  • Incumbents past performance in office.

2. The Image (Positive)

  • Some candidates are so personally admired that they attract votes from both parties.
  • Campaigns become marketing programs to project a candidates image.

2. The Image (Negative)

  • Politicians spend a fortune on negative ads.
  • Some go well beyond appropriate.

3. The Issues

  • Candidates stand on major issues.
  • Americans are polarized!

4. Past performance in office

    • In reality we vote based on retrospective voting not prospective.
      • We reelect incumbents when there is peace and prosperity.
      • Elect Challengers when we feel threatened and experience personal economic hardship.
      • Retrospective Voting is particularly important when voting for President.
